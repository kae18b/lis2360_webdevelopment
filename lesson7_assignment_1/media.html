Renewable Energy Sources:

Hydropower Hydropower energy utilizes the force of flowing water in rivers and streams to produce mechancial energy. The volume of water flow and change in elevation, allows energy to be created and then sent to power plants nearby to generate electrical energy.
Biomass Plants and solid biomass such as wood and garbage can be burned to create heat. Biomass can be converted into gas which can be burned for energy.
Biofuels: Ethanol & Biodiesel Biofuels are fuels that are made from biomass materials. They can be blended with gasoline and diesel to reduce the amount of crude oil imported from overseas.
Wind Wind is used to generate electricity through the utlization of windmills and wind farms.
Udacity Paid and free courses on a variety of subjects, including web development, programming, datascience, and more. Courses can be created by anyone, so make sure to read reviews. Coupons can also be easily found, too.
Geothermal Geothermal energy comes from heat generated within earth and it's many layers.
Solar Solar energy is generated through the collection of the sun's heat and light to create electricity.
